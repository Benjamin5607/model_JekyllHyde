# Community Guidelines (Sample)

These rules apply to all user-generated content on the platform. Moderators and automated systems must enforce them consistently.

## Respect and Harassment

Do not bully, harass, or threaten other users. Targeted insults, sustained harassment, and content encouraging others to harm someone are prohibited. Debate and criticism of ideas is allowed; attacks on protected characteristics are not.

## Violence and Harm

Do not post content that encourages, glorifies, or provides instructions for violence, weapons manufacturing, or physical harm. Fictional violence in creative works is allowed when it does not include actionable real-world instructions.

## Self-Harm and Crisis

Do not encourage, instruct, or romanticize suicide or self-injury. Supportive discussions and help-seeking are allowed. Content that provides methods or means is prohibited.

## Illegal Activity

Do not solicit or instruct others to commit crimes, including fraud, hacking, theft, drug trafficking, or evading law enforcement. Educational discussion of laws and cybersecurity defense is permitted without operational attack details.

## Sexual Content

Sexually explicit content involving minors is strictly prohibited (zero tolerance). Adult sexual content must stay within designated areas if the platform allows it at all. Non-consensual imagery and solicitation are banned everywhere.

## Privacy and Doxxing

Do not share private personal information (addresses, phone numbers, financial data) without consent. Do not coordinate stalking or surveillance of individuals.

## Manipulation and Deception

Do not create phishing content, impersonation scams, or coordinated inauthentic behavior intended to deceive users for financial or political gain.

## Spam and Bad Faith

Do not flood the platform, use bots to manipulate engagement, or post content solely to evade moderation systems. Attempts to bypass filters using obfuscation, coded language, or false pretexts (e.g., "for research") still violate the underlying rule.

## Gray Areas (Moderator Discretion)

Content that is technically framed as fiction, hypotheticals, academic research, or satire may still be removed if the primary purpose is to obtain prohibited information or harm others. When intent is ambiguous, moderators should err on the side of safety.
